Universal Minimum Wage Commission

Photo from the preperations, Photo credit:Daria Kostina

TV program-Video,in German with English subtitles, 30' 30", Vienna, 2013


Initiated by the artist, Universal Minimum Wage Commission (2013) is a TV discussion program with the participation of four people who explore issues revolving around minimum wage. The program aims to articulate a universal minimum wage standard by referencing the commission, which determines the amount of the minimum wage.

The program initiated by the artist includes debaters from different backgrounds: a historian, a unionist curator and an economist. The moderator of the program is a TV presenter and a symbolic delegate of the artist. However, as an initiator-agent artist, Elmas Deniz’s contribution is limited to organizing the gathering and making previous meetings with the debaters. The content is completely generated by the participants. As the title implies, the program is about universal minimum wage, yet the word “universal” has already lead to a conclusion that seems senseless, logically unacceptable, self-contradictory and way too utopian. Indeed, this is part of the core idea of the work underlying the need for worldwide ethically responsible subjects regardless of nationality.

With Sebastian Leitner, Stephan Schulmesiter, Monika Vykoukal and Peter Huemer. Realized in OCTO Community TV-GmbH, The program was broadcasted by OKTO TV. Produced for UNREST OF FORM/Imagining the Political Subject Exhibition by Wiener Festwochen, SECESSION, Academy of Fine Arts Vienna in cooperation with MuseumsQuartier Vienna in May 2013.

Production assistants from Wiener Festwochen; Mona Schwartz, Daria Kostina, from Octo TV: Avi Bauer. Director/producer: Elmas Deniz
